\# Vendor Corrective Action Framework  



\## Northstar Health Operations



\---



\# Purpose



This document defines the vendor corrective action framework for the Vendor Performance subsystem within Northstar Health Operations.



The purpose of this framework is to:



\- standardize operational vendor remediation processes

\- define escalation-driven intervention expectations

\- support operational continuity stabilization

\- support vendor recovery monitoring

\- support leadership escalation oversight

\- support operational accountability

\- support future corrective action reporting

\- support future Power BI operational recovery dashboards



This framework serves as the primary reference for managing operational response and remediation activity related to unstable vendor performance across the Northstar ecosystem.



\---



\# Operational Philosophy



Vendor corrective action processes should prioritize:



\- operational continuity

\- proactive intervention

\- escalation visibility

\- vendor accountability

\- measurable recovery expectations

\- leadership operational awareness



Corrective action governance should support:



\# operational stabilization



NOT:



\# punitive vendor management.



The framework is intended to establish consistent operational recovery expectations following vendor instability, SLA degradation, fulfillment failures, or escalation-heavy operational behavior.



\---



\# Corrective Action Objectives



The Vendor Corrective Action Framework is intended to support:



\- vendor recovery planning

\- escalation reduction

\- fulfillment stabilization

\- SLA improvement

\- operational continuity restoration

\- vendor accountability visibility

\- disruption mitigation

\- operational oversight consistency



The framework should help leadership:



\- identify recurring vendor instability

\- formalize remediation expectations

\- monitor vendor recovery progress

\- reduce operational disruption exposure

\- improve continuity-sensitive vendor relationships



\---



\# Corrective Action Triggers



Corrective action review may be initiated when operational thresholds or disruption indicators are identified.



Example trigger categories may include:



| Trigger Category | Example Operational Condition |

|---|---|

| SLA Breach Frequency | Repeated SLA failures within reporting period |

| Escalation Volume | Escalation-heavy vendor activity |

| Fulfillment Instability | Repeated delayed or partial shipments |

| Operational Impact Severity | High or critical operational disruption events |

| Corrective Action Recurrence | Repeated remediation dependency |

| Continuity Risk Exposure | Vendor instability affecting operational continuity |



These triggers are intended as:



\# operational governance examples



NOT:



\# rigid enterprise thresholds.



\---



\# Example Corrective Action Lifecycle



Example operational corrective action stages may include:



| Stage | Purpose |

|---|---|

| Identification | Vendor instability identified |

| Operational Review | Leadership and operational review initiated |

| Vendor Notification | Vendor formally notified of concerns |

| Remediation Planning | Recovery expectations established |

| Monitoring Period | Vendor recovery monitored over time |

| Reassessment | Recovery progress evaluated |

| Resolution or Escalation | Vendor stabilized or escalated for further review |
