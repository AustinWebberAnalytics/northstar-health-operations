\# Vendor Recovery KPI Framework  



\## Northstar Health Operations



\---



\# Purpose



This document defines the vendor recovery KPI framework for the Vendor Performance subsystem within Northstar Health Operations.



The purpose of this framework is to:



\- standardize vendor recovery performance measurement

\- support operational stabilization analysis

\- support escalation reduction monitoring

\- support remediation effectiveness visibility

\- support leadership recovery reporting

\- support operational continuity analysis

\- support future Power BI recovery dashboards

\- support future SQL-based recovery trend reporting



This framework serves as the primary reference for interpreting vendor remediation and operational recovery performance across the Northstar ecosystem.

\# Primary Recovery KPIs



\---



\# Corrective Action Resolution Rate



\## Purpose



Measures the percentage of corrective action cases successfully closed.



\## Formula



```text

(Closed Corrective Action Cases ÷ Total Corrective Action Cases) × 100

```



\## Interpretation



Higher resolution rates generally indicate:



\- effective remediation processes

\- operational stabilization

\- successful vendor recovery efforts



Lower resolution rates may indicate:



\- unresolved vendor instability

\- prolonged operational disruption exposure

\- ineffective remediation planning



\---



\# Escalation Reduction Rate



\## Purpose



Measures reduction in escalation frequency following remediation efforts.



\## Formula



```text

((Pre-Remediation Escalations - Post-Remediation Escalations) ÷ Pre-Remediation Escalations) × 100

```



\## Interpretation



Higher reduction rates generally indicate:



\- improved vendor coordination

\- operational stabilization

\- successful recovery intervention



Lower reduction rates may indicate:



\- recurring instability

\- ineffective remediation activity

\- persistent coordination issues



\---



\# SLA Recovery Improvement Rate



\## Purpose



Measures improvement in SLA compliance following corrective action activity.



\## Formula



```text

((Post-Recovery SLA Compliance - Pre-Recovery SLA Compliance) ÷ Pre-Recovery SLA Compliance) × 100

```



\## Interpretation



Higher improvement rates generally indicate:



\- successful operational recovery

\- improved responsiveness

\- stabilization of vendor operations



Lower improvement rates may indicate:



\- unresolved SLA instability

\- ineffective remediation efforts

\- ongoing operational disruption exposure



\---



\# Recovery Monitoring Duration



\## Purpose



Measures the average duration of corrective action monitoring periods.



\## Formula



```text

Total Monitoring Duration ÷ Total Corrective Action Cases

```



\## Interpretation



Shorter durations may indicate:



\- faster operational stabilization

\- efficient remediation execution



Longer durations may indicate:



\- recurring instability

\- prolonged recovery dependency

\- elevated operational oversight requirements



\---



\# Corrective Action Recurrence Rate



\## Purpose



Measures how frequently vendors require repeated corrective action intervention.



\## Formula



```text

(Vendors With Multiple Corrective Actions ÷ Total Vendors Under Corrective Action) × 100

```



\## Interpretation



Higher recurrence rates may indicate:



\- unstable vendor recovery

\- recurring operational failures

\- elevated continuity-risk exposure



Lower recurrence rates generally indicate:



\- sustainable remediation outcomes

\- improved vendor reliability
