# Workforce Analysis Framework



## Purpose



This document defines the analytical approach used to evaluate workforce capacity, staffing coverage, workload distribution, workforce utilization, and staffing-related operational risk.



The framework establishes how workforce data should be reviewed, interpreted, and reported to support operational planning and resource allocation decisions.



---



## Analytical Objectives



The Workforce Coordination subsystem is designed to support analysis in five primary areas:



- Workforce capacity

- Staffing coverage

- Workload distribution

- Workforce utilization

- Workforce risk



Together, these areas provide visibility into the organization's ability to meet operational demand.



---



## Workforce Capacity Analysis



Objective:



Evaluate available staffing resources and workforce availability.



Primary Questions:



- How many employees are available to support operations?

- Which departments have the greatest staffing capacity?

- Where are workforce shortages occurring?

- Are staffing resources aligned with operational demand?



Primary Dataset:



- workforce-roster



Primary KPIs:



- Active Workforce Count

- Workforce Availability Rate



---



## Coverage Analysis



Objective:



Evaluate workforce coverage across operational teams and shifts.



Primary Questions:



- Are planned coverage requirements being met?

- Which operational areas are experiencing coverage gaps?

- Where is backup coverage required?

- Which teams face the greatest coverage risk?



Primary Dataset:



- workforce-coverage-schedule



Primary KPIs:



- Coverage Fulfillment Rate

- Uncovered Shift Count



---



## Workload Analysis



Objective:



Evaluate how operational work is distributed across personnel and teams.



Primary Questions:



- Which employees carry the highest workloads?

- Where are workload imbalances occurring?

- Which teams may require workload redistribution?

- Are staffing resources being utilized effectively?



Primary Dataset:



- workforce-workload-distribution



Primary KPIs:



- Capacity Utilization Rate

- Overloaded Employee Count

- Underutilized Employee Count



---



## Assignment Analysis



Objective:



Evaluate workforce responsibilities and assignment distribution.



Primary Questions:



- How is work allocated across personnel?

- Which employees support multiple operational areas?

- Where are assignment concentrations occurring?

- Which assignments create coordination complexity?



Primary Dataset:



- workforce-assignments



Primary KPIs:



- Active Assignment Count

- Cross-Functional Assignment Rate



---



## Workforce Risk Analysis



Objective:



Identify workforce-related conditions that may negatively impact operations.



Primary Questions:



- What staffing issues require management attention?

- Which workforce risks remain unresolved?

- Where are staffing constraints affecting performance?

- Which operational areas face the greatest workforce-related risk?



Primary Dataset:



- workforce-escalations



Primary KPIs:



- Open Escalation Count

- High Severity Escalation Count



---



## Reporting Priorities



Workforce reporting should prioritize:



1. Staffing availability

2. Coverage gaps

3. Workload imbalances

4. Capacity utilization

5. Workforce-related operational risks



These areas provide the greatest visibility into workforce conditions that may impact operational performance.



---



## Interpretation Guidelines



Workforce metrics should be evaluated collectively rather than independently.



Examples:



- High workforce availability does not guarantee adequate coverage.

- Adequate coverage does not guarantee balanced workloads.

- Balanced workloads do not eliminate workforce-related operational risk.



Analytical conclusions should consider staffing capacity, workload conditions, coverage requirements, and escalation activity together to provide a complete view of workforce performance.



---



## Expected Outcomes



This framework supports identification of:



- Staffing shortages

- Coverage risks

- Workforce utilization trends

- Workload balancing opportunities

- Capacity constraints

- Workforce-related operational risks



The goal is to provide actionable workforce intelligence that supports informed operational planning and effective resource allocation decisions.
